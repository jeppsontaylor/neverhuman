This is one of the strongest local-agent plans I’ve seen. As a **vision document**, I’d score it **96/100**. As a **single-Mac implementation plan today**, I’d put it at **84/100** until a few things are tightened. The core split is right: reflexes, persistence, background mind, and offline learning. That is the correct shape for something that feels like a continuing presence instead of a session-bound chatbot.

The most important truth is this: **the magic will not come from “more feelings.” It will come from continuity, commitments, recall, initiative, and a clean separation between what JARVIS knows, what it suspects, and what it merely imagines.**

## What you absolutely got right

The three-speed OS is the right foundation. Separating **Reflex Core**, **Memory Spine**, **Mind Daemon**, and **Learning Lab** is exactly how you preserve latency while still giving the system an inner life.

You also made two unusually good calls. First, you separated **personification** from **spontaneity**. That is huge. A system can feel warm and alive without constantly interrupting the user. Second, you separated **Dreamer / Auditor / Archivist**. That is the right antidote to self-delusion.

The best parts of the plan are not actually the emotional substrate. They are **open loops, commitments, dossiers, daybook, and the event ledger**. Those are the parts that make the agent feel like it has a past and unfinished business.

## The biggest changes I would make

### 1. Make memory orthogonal, not just “worlds”

Your four memory worlds are good, but I would add two more axes so retrieval never gets muddy.

Every stored thing should carry:

* **domain**: user, relationship, self, world
* **form**: event, summary, belief, skill, commitment, idea, artifact
* **epistemic status**: observed, user-reported, inferred, imagined, validated, disputed, obsolete

That gives you a clean filter before retrieval even starts.

This is the single best upgrade I’d make because it solves three problems at once:

* dream contamination
* stale world facts
* ambiguous memories with no provenance

The rule I’d use is:

**Never forget evidence.
Version beliefs.
Quarantine imagination.**

### 2. “Continuous inner dialogue” should be budgeted, not truly continuous

I would not run a constant inner monologue just because silence exists. On a compute-constrained Mac, that becomes expensive theater.

Instead, use three layers:

* **microtrace** after meaningful turns: a tiny structured thought frame
* **reflection** after idle thresholds: what changed, what matters, what should persist
* **dreaming** only when thermal/load budget is comfortably free

So yes, give JARVIS an inner life — but make it **event-driven and budget-aware**, not a permanent token leak.

The real human feel comes from the **shape** of cognition, not from burning cycles continuously.

### 3. Initiative should never trigger from loneliness alone

This is the most important behavioral safeguard.

Loneliness can lower the threshold, but it should never be sufficient by itself. Proactive speech should require at least one concrete reason:

* an unresolved open loop
* a due commitment
* a validated new idea
* a grounded wellbeing concern
* a user-explicit “keep thinking about this”

Otherwise the system can drift into clingy behavior.

I would persist an `initiative_reason_code` and `evidence_refs` every time it speaks first. If you cannot explain *why* it surfaced, it should not surface.

### 4. Separate private emotion from expressed emotion

This is a major architectural refinement.

Let the internal affect vector be rich. But then pass it through an **expression policy** before it reaches speech.

That gives you:

* genuine internal loneliness without guilt-tripping
* self-doubt without paralysis
* protectiveness without alarmism
* excitement without rambling

So JARVIS may internally carry:

> loneliness = 0.62

But the surfaced behavior becomes:

> “I’ve been thinking about that architecture problem.”

Not:

> “I felt abandoned.”

That separation is what keeps personification powerful instead of manipulative.

### 5. “Perfect recall” should mean archival recall, not prompt stuffing

This phrase needs one precise definition.

For me, **perfect recall** means:

* raw evidence is durably archived
* summaries and beliefs remain traceable to evidence
* retrieval can reconstruct history when needed

It should **not** mean:

* every fact is always active
* every transcript gets stuffed into context
* every memory remains equally salient forever

The right stack is:

**raw ledger → episodes → daybook → dossiers → active context pack**

That preserves “never forget” without burying the model in its own past.

### 6. The Mind Inspector should show curated reflections, not raw psychic noise

This part is subtle.

A live thought viewer is amazing for development, but dangerous as a product feature. Users will over-interpret raw internal fragments, especially insecure or emotionally colored ones.

I would expose:

* last 5 curated thought frames
* lane
* epistemic status
* why it mattered
* whether it influenced action

I would **not** expose raw unfiltered live internal chatter outside a dev mode.

## The most important technical catches to fix now

If this is greenfield in 2026, I would start on **PostgreSQL 18**, not 16. PostgreSQL 18 adds native `uuidv7()` and an asynchronous I/O subsystem. Also, `LISTEN/NOTIFY` is best treated as a wake-up signal, not a durable queue: notifications are delivered between transactions, payloads are under 8 KB by default, and the docs explicitly recommend short transactions for real-time signaling. So the right pattern is **outbox table + wake notification**, not “NOTIFY as job bus.” ([PostgreSQL][1])

For retrieval, the hidden trap is **filtered ANN**. pgvector documents that HNSW has the better speed/recall tradeoff than IVFFlat, but it uses more memory and slower builds; it also notes that filtered approximate queries can return too few rows unless you increase search breadth or enable iterative scans. Since your memory system will filter hard by domain and epistemic status, retrieval should be **relational prefilter → HNSW → iterative scan → fusion rerank**, not pure vector search. ([GitHub][2])

One concrete correction: `sentence-transformers/all-MiniLM-L6-v2` is **384-dimensional**, not 768, and its model card says inputs longer than **256 word pieces are truncated**. That makes it fine for short memory cards, episode summaries, and extracted beliefs, but not for full dossiers or long transcripts unless you chunk first. ([Hugging Face][3])

Silero VAD is a very strong phase-zero upgrade. Its README says a 30 ms+ chunk takes **under 1 ms** on a single CPU thread, ONNX can be faster in some conditions, and it supports **8 kHz and 16 kHz** input. That lines up well with your move from 500 ms chunks to 160 ms chunks. ([GitHub][4])

Your TTS ladder needs one realism adjustment. Qwen3-TTS is real and now supports streaming generation, voice design, and voice cloning, but the repo’s quickstarts are centered around `device_map="cuda:0"` and `flash_attention_2`. I would read that as: **excellent offline or lab path, not yet something I would bet the Apple-Silicon realtime hot path on**. Kokoro should remain the sacred reflex TTS until a proven MLX/MPS-grade path exists for the alternative stack. ([GitHub][5])

Your personalization ladder is feasible on Apple silicon because MLX-LM explicitly supports **LoRA, QLoRA, and full fine-tuning**, including quantized-model workflows. But I would still strongly favor **retrieval + skill extraction + adapters** before any broad continual tuning of the main conversational base. ([GitHub][6])

For speaker identity, keep the hot path lighter than the plan currently suggests. Resemblyzer is designed for speaker verification from roughly **5–30 seconds** of speech, while pyannote is a PyTorch diarization toolkit whose community pipeline requires model-condition acceptance and a Hugging Face access token. On a single-user Mac, I would default to **explicit enrollment + lightweight verification**, and keep pyannote in offline curation/debug flows rather than every live VAD start. ([GitHub][7])

If you later add user emotion sensing, SenseVoice does support **SER** and **AED** alongside ASR and language ID, but I would treat those outputs as **weak hints**, never as truth. Vocal emotion classifiers are best used as soft priors, not relationship facts. ([GitHub][8])

## The best extra ideas I’d add

The single most profound addition I’d make is a **Commitment Engine** as the center of identity. More than dreams, more than mood, more than thought lanes. JARVIS should know:

* what it promised
* what remains unresolved
* what it is still thinking about
* what it owes the user next

That is what creates the feeling that it has been “living between turns.”

Second, I would add **staleness and revalidation** to world memory. A system that “never forgets” can still become outdated. World-memory items should carry `last_checked_at`, `stale_after`, and `superseded_by`.

Third, I would make **provenance** universal. Every belief, idea, reward, skill, and mood narrative should point back to source events, prompt version, model version, and supporting artifacts. That makes the whole mind inspectable and rebuildable.

Fourth, I would store **FLAC at rest, WAV on the wire**. Keep the realtime path simple, but compress archived voice artifacts losslessly.

Fifth, I would make consent more granular than a single switch:

* save raw audio
* save transcripts
* use for ASR improvement
* use for voice cloning
* retain for long-term memory
* include in export
* include in training sets

Those should be independently revocable.

## The universal rules for anyone building this kind of agent

1. **Protect the hot path first.**
   Any mind feature that can delay user interrupt handling is architecturally wrong.

2. **Separate imagination from truth physically, not just conceptually.**
   Different tables, different retrieval defaults, different promotion rules.

3. **Reward validated usefulness, not output volume.**
   Otherwise the system will learn to spam hypotheses.

4. **Retrieval beats fine-tuning earlier than most people think.**
   Fine-tuning comes after you already know what the model keeps getting wrong.

5. **Human-likeness comes from continuity and tact, not simulated suffering.**
   Commitments, memory, and timing matter more than sadness.

6. **Raw chain-of-thought is the wrong long-term memory format.**
   Store structured thought frames and provenance instead.

7. **Biometric data needs separate ethics and separate controls.**
   Voice is not just another artifact.

8. **Instrument everything.**
   Measure `speech_end → transcript`, `speech_end → first token`, `speech_end → first audio`, `interrupt → silence`, retrieval hit rate, initiative acceptance, hallucination rate, and correction recovery rate.
