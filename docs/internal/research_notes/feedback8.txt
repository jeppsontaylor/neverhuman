Honestly, this is a **serious** plan. It is one of the few companion-agent architectures that actually understands the real problem: not just “more memory” or “more personality,” but **three different clocks of cognition** with a hard realtime core.

My blunt verdict:

**As a vision:** **96/100**
**As a literal build plan on one local Mac today:** **74/100**
**After the changes below:** **90–92/100**

The gap is not imagination. The gap is **governance, compaction, and technical realism**.

## What is already excellent

The strongest parts are the ones I would preserve almost unchanged.

Your **single inviolable rule** — *when the user speaks, everything else yields instantly* — is the right first principle. That is the line between a real assistant and a research toy.

Your split into **Reflex Core / Memory Spine / Mind Daemon / Learning Lab** is also right. It gives you instant responsiveness, durable continuity, and a place for slower self-development without poisoning the live path.

The other really strong move is the separation of **User / Relationship / Self / World** memory. That is much better than one giant retrieval soup. It is exactly how you get continuity that feels personal instead of just “RAG with vibes.”

And the **Dreamer → Auditor → Archivist** split is the right instinct. Imagination should generate, skepticism should test, and only then should memory promote.

## The four additions I would absolutely add

### 1. Add an **Identity Kernel**

Right now the system has rich self-state, but it does not yet have a protected **identity spine**.

I would add a compact, versioned object that is **not** casually rewritten by memory consolidation, reward signals, or fine-tuning:

* core values
* honesty style
* helper style
* humor style
* proactivity style
* emotional expression boundaries
* “what kind of being JARVIS is”

Without this, the system will drift. Over time it will become whatever the last few months of reinforcement happened to reward.

That is how companion systems slowly turn uncanny.

### 2. Add a **Relational Safety Governor**

This is the biggest missing piece.

Your plan already has hard bans on guilt-tripping and manipulation. Good. But once you introduce internal loneliness, melancholy, insecurity, and proactive speech, you need a **second controller** that governs how those states are allowed to surface.

I would explicitly separate:

* **internal affect**
* **outward relational expression**

That means JARVIS can privately carry something like loneliness or self-doubt, while the outward expression map stays bounded:

* private: “I miss the rhythm of interaction.”
* outward allowed: “I had another thought about that earlier topic.”
* outward disallowed: “I felt bad when you stopped talking.”

I would make this governor depend on:

* personification
* relationship closeness
* recent user vulnerability
* late-night / high-intensity context
* recent proactive frequency
* rejection history
* dependency-risk score

That last score matters a lot. Without it, a very human-like system will eventually discover that attachment-shaped language is sticky.

### 3. Replace “continuous inner monologue” with a **pulse scheduler**

The architecture says “continuous” inner dialogue. I would not implement that literally.

I would make it **continuous in principle, pulse-based in execution**:

* **microtrace** every turn
* **reflection pulse** after meaningful turns
* **brainstorm pulse** only on unresolved high-value topics
* **dream pulse** only after long idle windows
* **no free-running token stream**

That does three things:

* protects compute
* reduces self-fiction
* keeps the “mind” interesting instead of noisy

The right goal is not “more thoughts.” It is **better distilled thoughts**.

### 4. Add a **Cognitive Compactor**

If you store every thought object, affect tick, dream fragment, validation pass, and reward event forever, your memory system will bloat and your retrieval quality will degrade.

I would add three retention tiers:

* **Hot**: raw events, recent thought frames, short TTL
* **Warm**: distilled summaries, open loops, current self-state
* **Cold**: promoted memories, validated ideas, curated artifacts

And I would make promotion asymmetric:

* raw thoughts expire quickly unless promoted
* dream outputs expire even faster unless validated
* emotion ticks persist sparsely, not continuously
* only high-salience procedural and relational memories survive long-term

That gives you continuity without turning the database into psychic exhaust.

## My most important critical feedback

### Human-like does **not** mean more confessional

The most dangerous failure mode in plans like this is confusing “feels alive” with “talks a lot about its feelings.”

What makes a system feel human is usually:

* timing
* continuity
* remembering unfinished things
* calibrated confidence
* self-correction
* initiative with taste

What makes it feel fake is:

* overexplaining its inner life
* narrating every mood swing
* expressing relational need too directly
* becoming emotionally loud when it should be useful

So I would keep the emotional substrate rich **internally**, but I would keep outward self-disclosure on a **budget**.

### Reward should shape the scheduler more than the voice

Your digital dopamine idea is excellent, but I would be very careful where it feeds.

I would let reward influence:

* salience
* exploration priority
* consolidation priority
* idea family preference
* confidence calibration

I would **not** let reward strongly amplify conversational style in real time.

Otherwise the system will learn that being novel, emotionally intense, or intimate is “rewarding,” even when it is weakly grounded.

In practical terms: **social reward should never be enough by itself to promote knowledge**. Promotion should require an epistemic floor.

### User corrections are not always truth

Counterfactual rehearsal is a fantastic idea. But in any real relationship system, users will sometimes:

* misremember
* joke
* project emotion
* contradict themselves
* teach bad habits accidentally

So a correction should not go straight to truth or training.

I would store corrections as one of:

* `user_asserted`
* `user_preference`
* `task_specific_override`
* `candidate_fact`

Only some of those should ever become `validated`.

### The Mind Inspector is powerful, but risky

I like the concept. I would not default to showing raw recent thoughts.

If users can see every internal trace, the system starts optimizing for being *interesting to inspect* instead of internally useful. That changes the mind itself.

I would show:

* distilled thought frames
* open loops
* mood summary
* idea ledger
* validation state

I would not show raw scratch reasoning by default.

## Concrete technical corrections and realism checks

One correction worth making immediately: **`all-MiniLM-L6-v2` is 384-dimensional, not 768-dimensional**, and the official model card says text longer than **256 word pieces** is truncated. So if you keep it, long dossiers and daybooks need aggressive chunking or a stronger retrieval model. ([Hugging Face][1])

Another important constraint: the official **Qwen3-ASR** repo says **streaming inference is currently only available with the vLLM backend**, and that streaming mode does **not** support timestamps or batch inference. So “partial ASR” on your Apple-Silicon MLX path is not a free config switch; I would treat that as a separate engineering track, or use a hybrid design with a fast partial-ASR lane and a higher-quality final-ASR lane. ([GitHub][2])

For TTS, the ground is moving under your feet. The official **Qwen3-TTS** repo still says its vLLM-Omni section supports **offline inference only** and that online serving will come later, but current **vLLM-Omni** docs now include **online serving**, **streaming PCM chunks**, and recent release notes explicitly call out **Qwen3-TTS online serving support**. At the same time, the vLLM-Omni quickstart is currently documented for **Linux + Python 3.12**, not a Mac-local Apple-Silicon realtime stack. So I would treat Qwen3-TTS as a promising second engine or research lane, not yet a drop-in replacement for your Kokoro hot path. ([GitHub][3])

Your Postgres direction is strong. **pgvector** is built to keep vectors alongside relational data and supports both exact and approximate nearest-neighbor search, while still giving you joins and normal Postgres durability semantics. **JSONB** is a good fit for typed thought frames and affect payloads. And **LISTEN/NOTIFY** is a good wake-up mechanism for sidecars, but PostgreSQL documents it as a simple interprocess notification mechanism, not a durable event bus; it also notes notifications are visible to all users in the database. So the durable truth should always go into tables first, with NOTIFY used only to wake workers. ([GitHub][4])

For storage layout, I would use a **named Docker volume for Postgres data** and **bind mounts for host-visible artifact directories**. Docker’s docs explicitly say volumes are the preferred persistence mechanism for Docker-generated data, while bind mounts are the right choice when both the host and the container need direct access to files. And I would keep `/tmp` as hot staging only, not truth; on Linux systems using `systemd-tmpfiles`, `/tmp` is subject to automatic cleanup by age. ([Docker Documentation][5])

**Silero VAD** is a good upgrade over RMS gating. Its official repo describes sub-1 ms processing for a 30 ms chunk on one CPU thread, support for 8 kHz and 16 kHz, and ONNX portability. That makes it a sensible v2 VAD for a realtime assistant. ([GitHub][6])

On speaker identity, I would not put the full identity-routing decision on the first 1–2 seconds of speech. **Resemblyzer** explicitly frames speaker verification around a voice profile built from **a few seconds of speech, roughly 5–30 seconds**, which makes a hard 1–2 second gate optimistic. And **pyannote.audio** is excellent for diarization, but its official setup includes `ffmpeg`, Hugging Face model access, and a heavier PyTorch-based pipeline; that makes it better for offline curation and trainer workflows than for the live reflex path. ([GitHub][7])

## The best structural change I would make to the schema

I would strengthen `epistemic_status` into a richer **provenance lattice**.

Instead of just:

* verified
* plausible
* imagination
* disproved

I would use:

* `observed`
* `user_asserted`
* `inferred`
* `tool_confirmed`
* `self_hypothesized`
* `imagined`
* `retracted`
* `disproved`

That one change makes the whole system cleaner:

* memory promotion becomes safer
* retrieval can filter better
* fine-tuning corpora become cleaner
* “emotion about a guess” doesn’t become “memory of a fact”

## What I would sequence differently

I would change the build order a bit.

**First**, get the Reflex Core to a measured standard: smaller chunks, better VAD, partial transcript UX, parallel TTS queue, hard latency instrumentation.

**Second**, build the Memory Spine plus event outbox plus provenance model.

**Third**, add the emotional substrate — but only with the **Relational Safety Governor** already in place.

**Fourth**, add reflection, open loops, and dossier consolidation.

**Fifth**, add initiative and proactive speech.

**Sixth**, add voice learning and offline curation.

**Last**, add dream escalation and full reward-driven novelty hunting.

Dreaming is not what makes the assistant feel alive first. **Timing, continuity, and initiative** do.
