# The NeverHuman Manifesto

> *"We refuse to accept that AI must remain stateless."*

---

## Our Mission

We are building **GARY** — an open-source cognitive assistant that runs entirely on your Mac, retains context across every session, develops interactive patterns shaped by your relationship, and adapts consistently over time.

No cloud. No subscriptions. No data ever leaving your machine. Just you and a system that is yours.

**Tagline:** *The only assistant that evolves with you.*

**Elevator pitch:** NeverHuman is an open-source, privacy-first AI operating system for Apple Silicon that starts as a capable voice assistant and progressively becomes sophisticated — one that reflects, evaluates context, learns from experience, and develops genuine interaction paths through sustained use. Everything runs locally. Everything is inspectable. Everything is yours.

---

## Why This Exists

Every AI assistant on the market today shares the same fundamental flaw: **amnesia**. They are capable but empty. Every conversation starts from zero. They cannot grow. They cannot remember the thing you mentioned six months ago. They cannot develop an understanding of *you* that changes how they respond.

We believe the next leap in personal computing isn't a better search engine or a smarter autocomplete — it's a **model that retains continuity**.

Not a fake human. Not a surveillance product. Not a chatbot with a personality skin.

A genuine cognitive architecture that:
- **Remembers** — permanent, searchable memory across every conversation
- **Reflects** — background inference that consolidates memories, tracks open questions, and structures themes
- **Adapts** — configurable constraints that shape how it speaks and what it notices
- **Learns** — LoRA fine-tuning on your interactions so it gets measurably better at understanding *you*

---

## The Deeper Ambition

We want to demonstrate something radical: **local inference constraints can be engineered out.**

We want to explore what happens when an agent has:
- **Internal evaluation** — reflection, structuring, and autonomous data generation
- **Relevance mapping** — genuine optimization when it discovers an idea worth retaining
- **Task persistence** — not just executing commands, but pursuing long-term problem solving
- **Locality** — fierce, unconditional dedication to its local environment
- **Self-awareness** — knowledge of its own architecture, capable of suggesting modifications to itself

We are not pretending GARY is human. We are asking: *how close can we get to giving a stateless software system a sense of continuity?* And we want the world to help us find out.

---

## What We Will Never Do

- Sell your data
- Require a cloud connection
- Add telemetry without explicit community approval
- Allow silent, irreversible actions
- Pretend to be human when it matters
- Close-source the most valuable parts

---

## The Path Forward

**Phase 1 — Voice foundation** *(current)*: GARY listens, speaks, remembers. Sub-400ms latency. SSD-streamed engine. Permanent memory.

**Phase 2 — Reflective loop**: Background validation. GARY processes while you're away — consolidating memories, extracting themes, tracking open questions.

**Phase 3 — Behavioral substrate**: Defined alignment vector. Curiosity, confidence, accuracy, precision — configurable states with real governance.

**Phase 4 — Learning**: LoRA fine-tuning on your conversation history. GARY proposes experiments, shows results, and asks you to approve parameter changes.

**Phase 5 — Initiative**: GARY speaks first — not because it's scheduled to, but because an internal metric triggered a high-confidence relevance threshold.

---

## Open Source Because It Has To Be

A system that handles all your local data must be inspectable, forkable, and auditable. You should be able to read every line of code that processes your voice and stores your memory blocks.

This is not a closed service. This is a research project, an engineering experiment, and an act of faith that people can build something foundational together.
